# 01 — Overview

## The South County Signal

**Tagline:** *Your weekly cheat sheet for living well below the Providence line.*

---

## Elevator Pitch

The South County Signal is a free weekly email newsletter that curates the best things to do in Southern Rhode Island — live music, food and drink specials, beach events, nightlife, arts, and family-friendly happenings — for millennials and hip empty nesters who don't want to scroll through six Facebook groups to figure out what's happening this weekend. We build an engaged local audience, then monetize through featured business listings, sponsored placements, and advertising.

## Mission

To become the go-to source for what's happening in Southern Rhode Island. Not the town calendar. Not the tourism board. The friend who always knows what's going on and isn't afraid to have an opinion about it.

## The Problem We Solve

South County has no single, curated, personality-driven source for weekly events and local happenings. Information is fragmented across Facebook groups, individual venue Instagram accounts, town calendars, and word of mouth. Our readers are busy adults who want someone to do the work of aggregating, filtering, and recommending — then deliver it to their inbox every Thursday.

## Target Audience

**Primary:** Millennials (30–44) and Gen X empty nesters (45–60) living in or regularly visiting Southern Rhode Island. They have disposable income, enjoy going out, care about supporting local businesses, and are digitally savvy but time-poor.

**Secondary:** Weekend visitors from Providence, Connecticut, and Boston who come for the beaches, restaurants, and nightlife.

**Psychographic Profile:**
- Values experiences over things
- Prefers curated recommendations over raw information
- Drinks craft beer or natural wine, not Bud Light (but won't judge)
- Comfortable at both a dive bar and a farm-to-table dinner
- Appreciates humor, irreverence, and honesty
- Wants kid-friendly options *sometimes* but also wants to go out without the kids

## Geographic Coverage

**Primary (every issue):**
Narragansett, Wakefield / South Kingstown, Westerly / Watch Hill, Charlestown, Matunuck, Point Judith, North Kingstown, East Greenwich, Wickford

**Secondary (when compelling):**
Providence — notable concerts, restaurant openings, major cultural events, festivals worth the 30–45 minute drive

## Revenue Model Summary

The newsletter is free to subscribers. Revenue comes from local businesses paying for visibility within the newsletter:

1. **Featured listings** — Businesses pay to be spotlighted in specific sections
2. **Display advertising** — Banner, mid-roll, and footer ad placements
3. **Sponsored deals** — Businesses pay to feature exclusive offers to our readers
4. **Seasonal guides** — Sponsored annual/seasonal content (Summer Guide, Holiday Guide, etc.)
5. **Affiliate revenue** — Ticket platform commissions, local partnership rev-share

See `03-BUSINESS-MODEL.md` for full revenue projections and `07-MONETIZATION-GUIDE.md` for the ad inventory map.

## What Makes This Different

1. **Voice.** We have a point of view. The newsletter reads like your most plugged-in friend, not a municipal press release.
2. **Curation.** We don't list everything. We pick the 8–10 best things and tell you why they matter.
3. **Consistency.** Same format, same day, every week. Readers know exactly what to expect.
4. **Local-first monetization.** We sell to the businesses our readers already love, creating a flywheel where the ads are actually useful.
5. **Low overhead, high margin.** One person can run this. The content is free (events are public). The tool costs are under $100/month.
