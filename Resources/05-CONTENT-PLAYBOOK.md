# 05 — Content Playbook

## The Weekly Formula

Every issue follows the same 8-section structure. Consistency is the product — readers should know exactly what to expect and where to find it.

---

### Section 1: THE OPENER
**Label:** None (it's the opening paragraph)
**Purpose:** Hook them. Set the vibe for the week.
**Length:** 2–3 sentences
**Tone:** Conversational, seasonal, insider-y

**Rules:**
- Reference the weather, the season, a local moment, or a trending local topic
- No links in this section — it's pure voice
- Write it last (after you've assembled the rest of the issue, you'll know what tone to set)

**Example openers by season:**

*Spring:* "The vernal equinox hits Friday and South County is already acting like it's spring. The Knick is back, the St. Pat's energy is building all week, and if you've been hibernating since January — this is your week to come out."

*Summer:* "The ocean hit 68° this week and the Gansett parking lots are filling up by 9am again. Summer's not waiting for the calendar — and neither should you."

*Fall:* "First hoodie weather of the year. The tourists are gone, the locals have their beach back, and the restaurant scene gets interesting again. Here's what's worth your time."

*Winter:* "Look, January in South County is quiet. But quiet doesn't mean dead. There are exactly four things worth leaving the house for this week, and every single one of them is indoors with a drink in hand."

---

### Section 2: THIS WEEK'S MOVE (Headliner)
**Label:** `THIS WEEK'S MOVE`
**Purpose:** The single best event happening this week
**Length:** Event name + date/time/venue + 3–4 sentence description + CTA button
**Tone:** Enthusiastic, specific, persuasive
**Visual:** Dark card (ocean blue background), gold accents

**Rules:**
- This is THE pick. If someone only reads one thing, this is it.
- Lead with why it matters, not what it is
- Include a clear call-to-action ("Get Details →")
- Can be sponsored ("Presented by [Business]") — $150–250/week

**Writing formula:** [Why this matters] + [What makes it special] + [Who should go / what to expect] + [Urgency if applicable]

---

### Section 3: THE LINEUP (Roundup)
**Label:** `THE LINEUP` / "This Week in South County"
**Purpose:** The curated list of the week's best events
**Length:** 5–8 picks, each with category tag + name + date/time/venue + 1–2 sentence description
**Tone:** Punchy, varied, opinionated

**Category rotation (aim for 4–5 different categories per week):**
- 🟣 Live Music — concerts, open mics, DJ nights
- 🔴 Food & Drink — happy hours, wine dinners, tastings, pop-ups
- 🟢 Beach & Outdoor — surf events, cleanups, paddleboard, farmers markets, hikes
- 🟡 Arts & Culture — gallery openings, theater, comedy, film screenings
- 🔴 Nightlife — bar events, trivia, karaoke, dance nights
- 🔵 Family Friendly — events that are cool for adults but also work with kids
- 🟢 Fitness & Wellness — outdoor yoga, runs, group fitness

**Rules:**
- One "Featured" (sponsored) slot allowed per issue — marked with a "Featured" tag
- Don't list more than 2 events at the same venue
- Vary the geography — don't let Westerly or Narragansett dominate every week
- Don't include events that are already sold out unless there's a waitlist
- Link every event name

---

### Section 4: FOOD & DRINK SPOTLIGHT
**Label:** `WHERE TO EAT & DRINK THIS WEEK`
**Purpose:** Profile one restaurant, bar, brewery, or food truck
**Length:** Name + town + 3–4 sentence description
**Tone:** Warm, specific, personal recommendation
**Visual:** Warm gradient card with gold left border

**Rules:**
- Focus on what makes it worth going NOW — new menu, seasonal special, hidden gem, underappreciated
- Write it like you're telling a friend at a party
- Rotate between types: restaurant → bar → brewery → food truck → coffee shop
- This is the primary paid feature slot ($100–200/week) — businesses can pay to be profiled
- Even when paid, write it in your voice. It has to feel genuine.

---

### Section 5: WORTH THE DRIVE (Providence)
**Label:** `WORTH THE DRIVE` + Providence badge
**Purpose:** 1–2 Providence events genuinely worth the 30–45 minute drive
**Length:** 1–2 picks, each with name + date/time/venue + 1–2 sentence description

**Rules:**
- **Only include when there's something genuinely compelling.** Skipping this section some weeks keeps it credible.
- Concerts at PPAC, The Strand, Columbus Theatre
- Major restaurant openings or pop-ups
- Festivals (WaterFire, PVDFest, etc.)
- Never include Providence events that have a South County equivalent

---

### Section 6: INSIDER DEAL
**Label:** `INSIDER DEAL`
**Purpose:** One exclusive or notable deal from a local business
**Length:** Business name + offer + details/restrictions + code
**Visual:** Dark card with deal code in dashed border box

**Rules:**
- Ideally exclusive to Signal readers (makes them feel like insiders)
- Include clear redemption instructions
- Paid placement ($50–100/week) — entry-level ad product for small businesses
- If no deal is available, skip the section rather than running something weak

---

### Section 7: SIGNAL NOISE
**Label:** `SIGNAL NOISE`
**Purpose:** Community building, engagement, personality
**Length:** 1–2 sentences + interactive element
**Tone:** Playful, provocative, fun

**Rotating formats:**
- **Poll:** "Best lobster roll in South County?" with 3 options
- **Hot take:** "Controversial opinion: Scarborough is overrated. Roger Wheeler is the better beach."
- **Debate:** "Narragansett vs. Matunuck: which is the real South County beach?"
- **Reader question:** "What's the one restaurant you'd take an out-of-towner to?"
- **Local news take:** Quick opinion on a local development (new business, road project, etc.)
- **Results:** Share last week's poll results with commentary

**Rules:**
- Always include an interactive element (poll buttons, "reply to this email," etc.)
- This drives engagement metrics — replies count as engagement in Beehiiv analytics
- Keep it light. This is not the place for heavy takes.

---

### Section 8: FOOTER
**Purpose:** Housekeeping, CTA, sponsor, legal
**Contents:**
- "Know something we should feature? Hit reply."
- Social media links (Instagram, Facebook, TikTok)
- "Advertise With Us" button → links to media kit
- Footer sponsor (if sold)
- Preferences / Unsubscribe / View in Browser links
