## Slot Details & Specs

### Slot A: This Week's Move Sponsor
- **What the advertiser gets:** "Presented by [Name]" tag above the headliner card, logo optional
- **What they provide:** Business name, optional logo, optional one-liner
- **We write:** The headliner event description (they don't control editorial)
- **Best for:** Venues, event producers, large restaurants, real estate agencies, banks
- **Minimum commitment:** 1 week (4-week packages available at 15% discount)

### Slot B: Featured Lineup Event
- **What the advertiser gets:** Their event included in The Lineup with a "Featured" badge
- **What they provide:** Event details (name, date, time, venue, link)
- **We write:** The 1–2 sentence description in our voice
- **Best for:** Venues promoting specific shows, restaurants with special events
- **Minimum commitment:** 1 week

### Slot C: Mid-Newsletter Display Ad
- **What the advertiser gets:** Centered card with "Our Sponsor" label, 2–3 sentences + link
- **What they provide:** Ad copy (we can write it for them) + link
- **Best for:** Businesses wanting broader brand awareness — not event-specific
- **Minimum commitment:** 4 weeks recommended (brand awareness builds over time)

### Slot D: Food & Drink Spotlight
- **What the advertiser gets:** Full feature profile in the Spotlight section
- **What they provide:** Business name, location, any specific angle they want highlighted
- **We write:** The 3–4 sentence profile in our voice after visiting or researching
- **Best for:** Restaurants, bars, breweries, food trucks, coffee shops
- **Minimum commitment:** 1 week (monthly packages at 20% discount)

### Slot E: Insider Deal
- **What the advertiser gets:** Dark card showcasing their deal with a redemption code
- **What they provide:** Offer details, redemption instructions, code, restrictions
- **Best for:** Any local business — lowest price point, great for first-time advertisers
- **Entry-level product:** Use this to get small businesses in the door, then upsell to Spotlight or Headliner

### Slot F: Footer Sponsor
- **What the advertiser gets:** "Presented By [Name]" at the bottom of every issue
- **What they provide:** Business name, optional tagline
- **Best for:** Organizations wanting persistent, low-key brand exposure — tourism boards, hospitals, banks, insurance agencies
- **Minimum commitment:** 4 weeks (monthly billing)

## Sales Strategy

### Target Advertiser Tiers

**Tier 1: First 5 Advertisers (Months 7–9)**
Small, relationship-based. These are the businesses you already go to.
- Ocean Mist, Matunuck Oyster Bar, Perks & Corks
- United Theatre, Courthouse Center for the Arts
- Local surf shops, yoga studios, boutiques
- **Approach:** Personal outreach, offer Insider Deal or Spotlight at launch pricing

**Tier 2: Recurring Monthly (Months 9–15)**
Businesses with ongoing marketing budgets who want weekly presence.
- South County Tourism Council
- Local real estate agencies
- Narragansett Beer / local breweries
- Shelter Harbor Inn, Weekapaug Inn
- **Approach:** Media kit + 4-week package pitch

**Tier 3: Premium & Seasonal (Year 2+)**
Larger organizations and seasonal sponsors.
- South County Hospital
- Washington Trust, Centreville Bank
- Auto dealers (Flood Ford, Balise)
- Rhode Island Tourism Division
- **Approach:** Custom packages, seasonal guide sponsorships, annual commitments

### Objection Handling

**"We already do Facebook/Instagram ads."**
→ "Those are interruption-based — users are scrolling past. Our readers opened an email, clicked through, and are actively looking for things to do. Your ad sits next to a curated recommendation they trust."

**"How many subscribers do you have?"**
→ Lead with engagement, not size. "We have [X] subscribers with a [Y]% open rate — that means [Z] people actually see your placement every week. In South County, that's the equivalent of a packed restaurant on a Saturday night."

**"Can I try it for one week?"**
→ "Absolutely. Most of our advertisers start with the Insider Deal at [$X] and upgrade after they see the response. No contracts, no minimums."

**"That's expensive for a newsletter."**
→ "A single Instagram boosted post costs $50–100 and disappears in 24 hours. A placement in the Signal sits in someone's inbox, gets opened an average of 1.6 times, and lives on our web archive. It's one of the most cost-effective ways to reach South County."

## Affiliate & Passive Revenue

### Beehiiv Ad Network
Once on the Scale plan, Beehiiv's ad network can place ads from their marketplace in your newsletter. Revenue is based on opens and engagement. Expected: $1–5 per 1,000 opens. Not life-changing, but it's free money.

### Eventbrite Affiliate
Link to events on Eventbrite using affiliate links. Commission: typically 5–10% of ticket price. Volume-dependent — most useful for large ticketed events.

### Local Business Partnerships
Negotiate rev-share deals: "We'll promote your wine dinner in exchange for 10% of ticket sales attributed to our link." Track with UTM parameters or unique discount codes.

## Sponsored Content Disclosure

FTC requires clear disclosure of sponsored/paid content. Our approach:

- Headliner sponsor: "Presented by [Name]" tag clearly visible above the card
- Featured lineup event: "Featured" badge next to the category tag
- Spotlight: Add a small "Partner Spotlight" tag if paid (vs. editorial pick)
- Mid-ad: "Our Sponsor" label
- Insider Deal: Inherently sponsored — the deal IS the ad
- Footer: "Presented By" label

See `14-LEGAL-CONSIDERATIONS.md` for full FTC compliance details.
