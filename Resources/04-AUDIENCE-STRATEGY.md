# 04 — Audience Strategy

## Target Personas

### Persona 1: "Weekend Warrior" (Primary)
**Demo:** 32–42, lives in South County, dual income, may have young kids
**Behavior:** Works remote or hybrid. Wants to make weekends count. Checks Instagram but doesn't have time to dig for plans. Drinks craft beer or wine. Goes out 2–3 nights a month. Values quality over quantity.
**Pain point:** "I know there's stuff happening but I never find out until it's over."
**We solve this by:** Being the one source they check every Thursday. Done.

### Persona 2: "Coastal Empty Nester" (Primary)
**Demo:** 48–60, kids are grown or in college, own their home in South County
**Behavior:** Has more disposable income and free time than they've had in 20 years. Wants to rediscover their town. Interested in arts, dining, music, dance nights. Not interested in "kids stuff" — finally.
**Pain point:** "We used to go out all the time. Now I don't even know what's open."
**We solve this by:** Curating adult-forward events — wine dinners, live music, theater, dance nights — with personality that doesn't talk down to them.

### Persona 3: "Providence Escapee" (Secondary)
**Demo:** 28–45, lives in Providence or Cranston, has friends/family in South County
**Behavior:** Comes down on summer weekends. Would come down more if they knew what was happening outside beach season. Follows RI food/drink scene.
**Pain point:** "Is there anything going on down there in March?"
**We solve this by:** The "Worth the Drive" section works in reverse — we show them South County is alive year-round.

## Growth Strategy

### Organic Tactics (Months 1–6)

**1. Instagram (@southcountysignal)**
- Post 3–5x per week: event highlights, food shots, beach/sunset photos, poll stories
- Use Stories for "this weekend" quick hits every Thursday/Friday
- Reels: 15–30 second event previews, "best of" lists, behind-the-scenes at local spots
- Goal: 1,000 followers by month 3, 3,000 by month 6

**2. Facebook Groups (Seed Strategy)**
- Join and participate in: South County RI Community, Narragansett Neighbors, Westerly/Pawcatuck Area, South Kingstown Community
- Share genuinely useful event info (not spam) 2–3x per week with a link to subscribe
- Engage in comments, be helpful, build reputation as "the events person"
- Create a South County Signal Facebook Page for broader reach

**3. Launch Partnerships (Critical)**
- Identify 10 local businesses willing to cross-promote at launch
- Offer them free featured placement for the first 4 weeks in exchange for promoting the newsletter to their customers
- Target: restaurants, breweries, venues, surf shops, yoga studios
- Ideal partners: Ocean Mist, Matunuck Oyster Bar, Perks & Corks, United Theatre, Fantastic Umbrella Factory

**4. In-Person Touchpoints**
- QR code sign-up cards at coffee shops, breweries, and surf shops
- Sign-up sheet at local events you attend
- "Subscribe for your free South County sticker" at farmers markets

**5. Cross-Promotion with Complementary Newsletters**
- Reach out to Providence-area newsletters, RI food bloggers, local media
- Offer mutual shoutouts or "we recommend" features

### Paid Growth (Months 6+, When Revenue Supports)

**6. Instagram/Facebook Ads**
- Target zip codes: 02879, 02882, 02891, 02813, 02874, 02852, 02818
- Creative: "Stop scrolling. Start going out." with local beach/bar imagery
- Budget: $100–300/month
- Target CPA: $1.50–3.00 per subscriber

**7. Event Sponsorship**
- Sponsor a table/booth at local events with a QR code sign-up
- Partner with race organizers, festival producers, farmers market managers
- Budget: $50–200 per event

## Churn Prevention

- **Consistency:** Never miss a Thursday send. Ever. Consistency is the product.
- **Quality filter:** Don't pad the newsletter with weak events. Readers trust us because we only include what's actually good.
- **Engagement hooks:** Signal Noise polls, reader replies, "tell us what we missed" footer CTA
- **Seasonal relevance:** Adjust the mix — more beach/outdoor in summer, more arts/food/nightlife in winter
- **Re-engagement:** Beehiiv's built-in win-back automations for inactive subscribers
