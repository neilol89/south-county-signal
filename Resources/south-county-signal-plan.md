# The South County Signal
### A Weekly Newsletter for Southern Rhode Island

---

## Brand Concept

**Name:** The South County Signal
**Tagline:** "Your weekly cheat sheet for living well below the Providence line."
**Voice:** Smart, slightly irreverent, warm. Like your most plugged-in friend who always knows what's happening this weekend. Not stuffy, not try-hard. Think: casual authority with a wink.

**Target Audience:**
- Millennials (30-44) living in or visiting South County / Southern RI
- Hip empty nesters who still want to *do things*
- Parents who want adult-friendly plans (some kid-friendly, some decidedly not)
- Weekend visitors from Providence, CT, and Boston who hit the beaches

**Geographic Coverage (Primary):**
Narragansett, Wakefield/South Kingstown, Westerly/Watch Hill, Charlestown, Matunuck, Point Judith, North Kingstown, East Greenwich, Wickford

**Geographic Coverage (Secondary):**
Providence — notable events only (concerts, restaurant openings, major cultural events worth the drive)

## Revenue Model

### Phase 1: Audience Building (Months 1-6)
- **Goal:** 2,500+ subscribers
- **Strategy:** Free weekly newsletter, consistent quality, social media cross-promotion
- **Revenue:** $0 (investment phase)
- **Costs:** Email platform (~$30-50/mo), time, occasional dining/event attendance for content

### Phase 2: Soft Monetization (Months 6-12)
- **Goal:** 5,000+ subscribers
- **Revenue Streams:**
  - Featured listings: $50-75/week (restaurants, bars, event venues pay for a "Spotlight" placement)
  - Affiliate links to ticket platforms (Eventbrite, etc.)
  - Sponsored "Pick of the Week" section: $100-150/week

### Phase 3: Full Monetization (Year 2+)
- **Goal:** 10,000+ subscribers
- **Revenue Streams:**
  - Premium ad placements (banner, mid-roll, footer): $150-400/placement depending on position

  - Seasonal guides (sponsored): $500-1,000 per guide
  - "Best of South County" annual guide (digital + print): Sponsored categories at $300-1,000 each
  - Exclusive deals/partnerships with local businesses (rev share)

## Weekly Newsletter Formula

The following is the repeatable section-by-section template to follow every single week. Consistency is the product.

### SECTION 1: THE OPENER
**Purpose:** Hook them immediately. Set the vibe for the week.
**Format:** 2-3 sentences, conversational, timely. Reference the weather, the season, a local inside joke, or a trending local topic.
**Example:** *"The ocean hit 68° this week and the Gansett parking lots are filling up by 9am again. Summer's not waiting for the calendar — and neither should you. Here's what's worth your time this week."*

### SECTION 2: THE HEADLINER (1 pick)
**Purpose:** The single best thing happening this week. The one event you'd tell your best friend about.
**Format:** Event name, date/time, location, 3-4 sentence write-up with personality. Include a link.
**Label:** "THIS WEEK'S MOVE"
**Ad Opportunity:** Can be sponsored ("Presented by [Local Business]") — $150-250/week

### SECTION 3: THE ROUNDUP (5-8 picks)
**Purpose:** The core value prop — a curated list of the best things happening this week.
**Format:** Each pick gets: Event name | Date/Time | Location | 1-2 sentence description with a link
**Categories to rotate through each week (aim for variety):**
- Live Music / DJ Nights
- Food & Drink specials (happy hours, wine dinners, tasting events)
- Beach & Outdoor (surf contests, beach cleanups, paddleboard yoga, farmers markets)
- Arts & Culture (gallery openings, theater, comedy shows, film screenings)
- Family-Friendly (but still cool — not just "kids stuff")
- Nightlife (bar events, trivia, karaoke, dance nights)
- Fitness & Wellness (outdoor yoga, runs, group fitness events)

**Label:** "THE LINEUP"
**Ad Opportunity:** One "Featured Event" slot within the roundup — marked as sponsored — $75-125/week

### SECTION 4: THE FOOD & DRINK SPOTLIGHT
**Purpose:** Highlight one restaurant, bar, brewery, or food truck. Could be a new opening, a seasonal menu drop, or just a hidden gem worth revisiting.
**Format:** Name, location, what makes it worth going NOW, 3-4 sentences. Photo if possible.
**Label:** "WHERE TO EAT & DRINK THIS WEEK"
**Ad Opportunity:** This is the primary paid feature slot — businesses pay to be profiled here — $100-200/week

### SECTION 5: THE PROVIDENCE WORTH-THE-DRIVE (when applicable)
**Purpose:** 1-2 Providence events that are genuinely worth the 30-45 minute drive. Big concerts, restaurant openings, cultural events, festivals. Skip this section if nothing qualifies — don't force it.
**Format:** Event name, date/time, venue, 1-2 sentences on why it's worth leaving South County.
**Label:** "WORTH THE DRIVE"
**Note:** Only include when there's something genuinely compelling. Skipping this section some weeks keeps it credible.

### SECTION 6: THE DEAL
**Purpose:** One exclusive or notable deal, discount, or promotion from a local business. Makes readers feel like they're getting insider access.
**Format:** Business name, the deal, any restrictions, how to redeem.
**Label:** "INSIDER DEAL"
**Ad Opportunity:** Paid placement — businesses pay to feature their deal — $50-100/week. This is also a great entry-level ad product for small businesses.

### SECTION 7: THE HOT TAKE (optional, rotating)
**Purpose:** Build personality and community. A quick opinion, poll, or question. Could be: best lobster roll ranking, a debate (Narragansett vs Matunuck beach), a seasonal rant, or a reader poll.
**Format:** 2-3 sentences + a question or poll link. Drives replies and engagement.
**Label:** "SIGNAL NOISE"
**Note:** Rotate formats — some weeks it's an opinion, some weeks a reader poll, some weeks a quick local news take. This section builds brand loyalty and voice.

### SECTION 8: THE FOOTER
**Purpose:** Housekeeping + ad space.
**Contents:**
- Social media links
- "Know something we should feature? Reply to this email."
- "Want to advertise? [Link to media kit / rate card]"
- Unsubscribe link
- Footer sponsor logo/message

**Ad Opportunity:** Footer sponsor — $75-100/week. Low-key but persistent brand exposure.

## Brand Guidelines (Brief)

**Colors:**
- Primary: Deep ocean blue (#1B4965)
- Accent: Sandy gold (#D4A843)
- Background: Off-white (#FAF8F5)
- Text: Charcoal (#2D2D2D)

**Fonts:**
- Headlines: Bold serif (think: editorial, magazine-quality)
- Body: Clean sans-serif for readability on mobile

**Photography Style:**
- Warm, golden-hour lighting
- Candid over posed
- Local landmarks and textures (weathered wood, beach grass, oyster shells, boat hulls)
- Never stock-photo generic
