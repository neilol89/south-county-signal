# 09 — Outreach Templates

## Cold Outreach to Local Businesses

### Template 1: The Warm Intro (for businesses you actually go to)

**Subject:** Quick question about [Business Name]

Hey [First Name],

I'm Neil — I live in [Town] and I'm a regular at [Business Name]. I'm launching a weekly email newsletter called The South County Signal that curates the best events and things to do in Southern RI for locals who actually want to go out and do stuff.

I'm reaching out because I think your [specific thing — event nights / seasonal menu / live music calendar] would be a perfect fit for our readers. We feature one restaurant or bar each week in a dedicated spotlight section, and I'd love to include [Business Name] in an upcoming issue.

For our first month, I'm offering free featured placements to a handful of local businesses I personally believe in. No catch — just trying to build something useful for South County and I want to start with the places I already love.

Would you be open to a quick 10-minute chat this week? I can also just send over more details by email if that's easier.

Thanks,
Neil
The South County Signal
southcountysignal.com

### Template 2: The Value-First Pitch (for businesses you don't have a relationship with)

**Subject:** Reaching [X] South County locals every week

Hi [First Name],

I'm Neil, founder of The South County Signal — a free weekly email newsletter that curates the best events, dining, and things to do in Southern Rhode Island.

We reach [X] engaged subscribers every Thursday, with a [Y]% open rate (about 2x the industry average). Our readers are millennials and active adults in Narragansett, Wakefield, Westerly, and surrounding towns — exactly the people looking for a place to eat, drink, or go out this weekend.

I wanted to reach out because [specific reason — "your wine dinners are exactly the kind of thing our readers love" / "I saw you just launched a new spring menu" / "your live music calendar is stacked"].

We have a few advertising options starting at $25/week — from featuring a special deal to a full business profile. I'd love to send over our media kit or jump on a quick call to see if there's a fit.

Would either of those work for you?

Best,
Neil
The South County Signal

### Template 3: The Launch Partner Pitch (pre-launch or early stage)

**Subject:** Launching something new for South County — want to be part of it?

Hey [First Name],

I'm building a weekly email newsletter for South County called The South County Signal. Think of it as the one email that tells people what's actually worth doing this week — music, food, events, nightlife, all curated with a local voice.

I'm hand-picking 10 local businesses to be founding partners. Here's the deal:

- You get free featured placement in the newsletter for our first 4 weeks
- In exchange, you help spread the word — a sign-up card on your counter, a mention on your social media, or an email to your list
- After the first month, you'll have seen the newsletter, the audience, and the engagement — and you can decide if you want to continue with a paid placement

I'm only reaching out to businesses I'd personally recommend. [Business Name] is one of them.

Interested? I can send more details or swing by this week.

Neil
The South County Signal

## Follow-Up Sequences

Happy to send the one-page media kit if that's easier than a call. Just say the word.

Neil

Cheers,
Neil

Neil

## Post-Sale Communication

### Template: Confirming a Placement

**Subject:** You're in this week's Signal — here's what to expect

Hey [First Name],

Great news — [Business Name] is confirmed for this Thursday's issue of The South County Signal. Here's what we've got:

**Placement:** [Slot name — e.g., Insider Deal]
**Issue:** Week of [Date], Issue #[X]
**What readers will see:** [Brief summary of the copy — e.g., "Buy One Get One Free cocktails at Perks & Corks, valid Mon–Thu, code SIGNAL2FOR1"]

The newsletter goes out Thursday morning at ~9:00 AM EST to [X] subscribers. I'll send you a link to the live issue plus engagement stats (opens, clicks) by Friday afternoon.

If anything needs to change before Thursday, just reply to this email by Wednesday 5PM.

Thanks for being part of the Signal.

Neil

## Partnership & Cross-Promotion Templates

### Template: Cross-Promotion with Another Newsletter

**Subject:** Cross-promo idea — South County Signal x [Their Newsletter]

Hey [Name],

I run The South County Signal, a weekly events newsletter for Southern Rhode Island ([X] subscribers, [Y]% open rate). I came across [Their Newsletter] and think our audiences would overlap nicely.

Would you be interested in a mutual shoutout? Here's what I'm thinking:

- We mention [Their Newsletter] in our next issue with a subscribe link
- You mention The South County Signal in your next issue

No money changes hands — just a value exchange for both of our audiences. We've found our readers love discovering complementary newsletters.

Let me know if you're interested and I'll draft a blurb for your approval.

Neil
