THE SOUTH COUNTY SIGNAL

Your Weekly Cheat Sheet for Southern RI
Week of May 14, 2026 · Issue #4
Three things we teased last week are all happening this week — the drive-in opens, Eight to the Bar plays the Knick, the Dirty Dozen Brass Band rolls through East Greenwich — and somehow that's still not the most stacked Friday on the calendar. Summer in South County doesn't start on a calendar date. It starts the night Wuskenau Beach turns into a parking lot, the projector flips on, and the first opening notes of Jaws come through the windshield. Buckle up.
THIS WEEK'S MOVE
Misquamicut Drive-In Season Opener — Jaws on the Beach
Friday, May 15 · 9:00 PM (gates 6:30 PM, previews 8:00 PM) · Wuskenau Beach Pondside, Westerly
The Misquamicut Drive-In is one of the last working drive-ins in New England, and the night it flips on for the season is a South County summer ritual — the unofficial start to the months that matter. Opening with Jaws is the kind of cosmic alignment between movie and venue you don't get twice. One ticket per carload, first-come parking, gates at 6:30, previews at 8, sharks at 9. Bring blankets, bring snacks, leave the convertible at home. The drive-in is back. So is summer.

THE LINEUP
This Week in South County
Live Music
Dirty Dozen Brass Band
Thursday, May 14 · Evening · Greenwich Odeum, East Greenwich
Forty-five years of GRAMMY-winning brass-band-meets-funk-meets-bebop in a 400-seat room — the band that invented the phrase 'musical gumbo' and meant it. New Orleans walks into East Greenwich and turns Thursday into a school night you'll regret in exactly the right way.
Food & Drink
The Delightfully Respectful Celebration of the Nation's 50 States
Thursday, May 14 · Doors 5:00 PM · Westerly Armory, Westerly
BBQ chicken and pork, mac & cheese, salad, Toll House cookies, and a guided tour through all fifty states from Roberta Mudge Humble of the RI Heritage Hall of Fame. Twenty-eight bucks, ages ten and up, reservations at 401.596.8554. The weirdest, warmest, most Westerly Thursday on the schedule — and we mean that as a compliment.
Live Music
Dance Night at the Knick — Eight to the Bar + UNITED Big Band Album Release
Friday, May 15 · 8:00 PM · Knickerbocker Music Center, Westerly
Eight to the Bar has been moving dance floors with swing, boogie-woogie, and Motown for forty-plus years. They share the stage with the UNITED Big Band and Jazz Combo for an album release party, and the Knick's Starlight Ballroom is the exact room this music was built for. Wear shoes you can move in.
Nightlife
LaMP — Russ Lawton, Scott Metzger & Ray Paczkowski
Friday, May 15 · 9:00 PM · Ocean Mist, Matunuck
Russ Lawton and Ray Paczkowski are Trey Anastasio's rhythm section. Scott Metzger plays in JRAD. The three of them at the Mist on a Friday night is not the kind of show that usually happens at a beach bar — and the deck on a clear May evening is reason enough on its own. This will draw.
Arts & Culture
SCAA 'Quality Seconds' Pottery & Art Sale
Saturday, May 16 · 9:00 AM · South County Art Association, South Kingstown
Once a year, the South County Art Association sells slightly-imperfect pottery and member artwork at a fraction of gallery price. Collectors line up at nine. If you've been putting off buying real art for your kitchen wall — and you have been — this is the morning.
Live Music
Mark Cutler & Men of Great Courage
Saturday, May 16 · 9:30 PM (Doors 9:00 PM) · Pump House Music Works, Wakefield
Mark Cutler has been an RI rock institution since the Schemers, and Men of Great Courage is the project he's pouring his catalog into now. $15 advance, $20 at the door. Wakefield's late-Saturday move.
Live Music
The Wailers — 50 Years of Positive Vibrations
Sunday, May 17 · 8:00 PM · United Black Box, Westerly
Fifty years of Wailers. Two hundred seats. The Black Box turning into a reggae club on a Sunday night, performing Bob Marley's Rastaman Vibration end to end. The math is unfair to the band — this room is way too small for what they do — and that's exactly why you want to be in it.
Beach & Outdoor
RI Grown Farmers Market at Fishermen's Memorial
Sunday, May 17 · 8:30 AM – 12:00 PM · Fishermen's Memorial State Park, Narragansett
The Sunday market is officially open for the season. RI seafood, asparagus, the first strawberries if you're lucky, eggs, flowers, the works. Post-Wailers brunch fuel — or the whole Sunday if you read the room right.
WHERE TO EAT & DRINK THIS WEEK
The Olympia Tea Room
Westerly · 74 Bay St, Watch Hill
Open in Watch Hill since 1916 — the kind of sentence that gets thrown around carelessly but this one really has the years to back it up. The room is the draw: long bar, old wood, the way Bay Street looks through the windows in late spring. The kitchen does classic American with a strong seafood lean, the bartenders know the regulars by drink, and the patio comes back to life this month. Ten minutes from Wuskenau if you're building a full evening around Jaws — early dinner here, then over to the drive-in. Reservations smart on weekends.
WORTH THE DRIVE Providence
Newport Oyster & Chowder Festival
Saturday May 16 – Sunday May 17 · All day · Bowen's Wharf
Rhode Island's entire oyster industry on one wharf, plus award-winning chowders from kitchens across the state, live music, signature cocktails. Yes, it's Newport in May — but the weather window is exactly right, the parking is doable if you go early, and this is the rare festival where you actually leave full.
